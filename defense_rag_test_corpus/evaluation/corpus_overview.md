## Designed Retrieval Challenges

1. **Cross-document relationships**  
   Program names, suppliers, facilities, milestones, part numbers, and contract IDs recur across multiple files in each scenario.

2. **Versioning and ambiguity**  
   The corpus contains draft vs final language, updated schedules, revised budgets, similar supplier names, and reused acronyms.

3. **Multi-hop reasoning**  
   Many questions require combining a narrative source with a spreadsheet or tracing a cause from draft notes into a formal change package or test summary.

4. **Precision / recall stress**  
   Some documents are highly relevant; others are plausible distractors because they share similar names, parts, or schedule language.

5. **Spreadsheet-heavy retrieval**  
   Each workbook has multiple tabs with interrelated cost, schedule, risk, staffing, or scoring data.
