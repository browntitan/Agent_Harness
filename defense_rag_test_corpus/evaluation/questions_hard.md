# Hard Evaluation Questions

Each question is labeled with a stable question ID.

## H01
Explain why Asterion CDR moved and identify the documents that show both the emerging problem and the final approved answer.

## H02
Which Asterion cost increase is directly attributable to the approved change, and which concern remained a separate residual risk?

## H03
Why is it inaccurate to describe the Iron Vale endurance miss as only a software problem?

## H04
Did the proposed Iron Vale retrofit eliminate all schedule risk to limited user test? Explain why or why not.

## H05
Reconcile why Trident Echo’s cost increased to $23.31M and why shock qualification still only conditionally passed.

## H06
If someone says Blue Mica Wave 2 slipped because the hardware was bad, what is the better evidence-based answer?

## H07
Trace why the Harbor Scribe pilot moved from 22 Jul 2028 to 19 Aug 2028, and explain why leadership still considered the program worthwhile.

## H08
Which Ember Reach supplier won on best value, which one looked strongest technically, and what evidence shows the winning choice still needed post-award correction?

## H09
Differentiate North Coast Systems LLC from Northcoast Signal Labs and name the program each one supports.

## H10
Differentiate Halcyon Foundry from Halcyon Microdevices and explain which one had manufacturing void issues versus procurement scoring.

## H11
If one Asterion document says $47.80M and another says $49.58M, which figure is latest and why?

## H12
Does the Aegis Mobility Works proposal by itself prove Iron Vale can now meet the original endurance requirement?

## H13
Did Trident Echo’s original shock milestone move because of the test-result issue or because of manufacturing constraints?

## H14
Which artifacts best support a question about Blue Mica’s cost split and the operational rationale for adding trainers?

## H15
