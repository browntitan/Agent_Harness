NARRATIVE COMMENTARY

The action log is intentionally terse, but it captures how the program office translated factory concerns into ownership. CAR-22 became the primary quality vehicle for the foundry issue after inspectors identified recurring void indications in cabinet housings. Internal emails repeatedly asked whether the voids were 'cosmetic or structural.' The quality team treated them as structural until proof existed otherwise, which is why the program did not allow housings to flow unchecked into the shock build.

A secondary but important thread concerned bracket BR-6. At the time of this log the bracket had not yet failed any test. The team simply recognized that schedule margin was so thin that any structural weakness discovered in shock would be painful. For that reason, the structures group was told to keep a reinforcement concept warm. Later documents show that this precaution was justified.

CAR-22 SUMMARY

Corrective Action Record CAR-22 was opened against Halcyon Foundry for inconsistent internal quality on the cabinet housing castings. The problem statement cited unacceptable void indications, variable cleanup effort, and late notifications that prevented smooth factory sequencing. Proposed containment at the time of the log included 100 percent radiographic review of the next shipment, supplier-side sign-off by a foundry quality manager, and priority freight for acceptable castings. Long-term corrective action was expected to include process parameter tightening at pour and cooling stages.

This log is useful because it connects the supplier quality story to the later shock qualification narrative. A query phrased as 'what quality action was associated with the housing problem before the test brief?' should land here, not only in the more polished final documents.
